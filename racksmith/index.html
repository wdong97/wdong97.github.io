Straight talk

It matches the treatment. It doesn't steal the recording.

Here's what other tools won't tell you: a recording can't reveal the exact knobs behind it. Dozens of settings sound identical, so Racksmith doesn't sell you a magic "original preset." It hands you a rack that does the same job, one you can open, read, and make your own.

What it does

  • Rebuilds the treatment (tone, dynamics, space) as an editable rack.
  • Uses stock devices only, so a collaborator can open your project.
  • Labels every device, so you learn the move instead of renting it.
  • Hands you a real file. No lock-in, no subscription to hear your own sound.

What it won't do

  • Copy the original recording. That's sampling, and not ours to give.
  • Claim the artist's exact settings. That number doesn't exist.
  • Fake a full synth patch. For a raw sound it samples the note instead.
  • Bluff effects it can't hear clean. Guitar amps and dense mixes stay honest.
